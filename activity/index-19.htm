Student Council Induction Ceremony 

                "Do all you can, with all you have, where you are"
                                                                - President Roosevelt

On the 8th of August, 2018 Notre Dame Student Council Induction Ceremony was auspiciously conducted. It was a very prayerful and solemn affair. The ceremony started with the lighting of the Holy lamp accompanied with prayer songs. the students were administered oath by the Principal Sr.Mary Alice and with great honour were given their respective badges. The choir inspired one and all with martial beats and songs with zest. Bhavya Tyagi (Secretary) proposed the vote of thanks.

Congratulations to the student council.

Lesson for life!

“Do to others as you wish others to do unto you”

On 18th of July, Divine charitable trust (Blind school) presented a show here at Notre Dame School grounds to raise funds for the differently abled students.

Mr.Vincent the organizer with his team of blind students started the program with a prayer song and later entertained the students with Bollywood numbers. The students applauded so that the participants could hear and be encouraged.

Mr.Vincent urged the students to sharpen their skills and potentialities. He urged students to be prayerful and work for charity in their own small ways.

Sr.Mary Alice and the Head Boy, Aaron Justine of Std.XII A thanked the trust for their noble work.

Notre Dame student represents India in IFLC!

“Mile sur mera thumhara………………………”

In the words of Durga Acharya of Std.IX C –

“I wish to share my experience of participating in IFLC i.e, International Festival for Language and Culture. I had the opportunity of visiting three countries Belgium, Romania, and Germany. I was a part of the cultural delegation there. representing my country India. It was an enriching experience for me, I learnt about the culture, language, music and dance different countries.

This year 16 countries participated in the IFLC (Colours Festival). It was a great learning experience for me. We were different, yet it was our music that was the binding factor. I made many new friends, met great musicians and world leaders.


I want to firstly thank God, for giving me this opportunity. I would also like to thank Sister Mary Alice for her support and encouragement. My big thank you, to all my teachers for having faith in me. Winston Sir, for his love and constant encouragement, my mother for her love and guidance and lastly, my deep gratitude and regards to my Guru, my baba (my father) without his guidance, I would be nowhere. His training, emotional support and teachings made me confident to represent my country in true Indian spirit.”

Well done Durga…. You will go a long way!
