Silver Jubilee Celebrations…….Glorious 25 years and beyond……

Celebration of life
Celebration of victory over challenges
Celebration of watching things taking shape all these years.

With grateful heart, immense faith and boundless joys, Notre Dame family, B.T.P.S., celebrated its Silver Jubilee on 29th of November 2015, in the Millennium ground of N.T.P.C.

The School was honoured to have Shri Umesh Prasad Pani, the director, HR and Commercial, N.T.P.C. as the Chief guest. Rev.Sr.Mary Beena , the Provincial Superior of the Sisters of Notre Dame was present as Guest of Honour. Sister is a person of great vision and acumen. Mrs.Geeta Pani had greatly gladdened us as being a guest of honour. She is an Odissi dancer of international renown and is intimately involved in the welfare activities in the different campuses of N.T.P.C. Amongst the various dignitaries, officers from N.T.P.C and all the previous principals of the institution – Sr.Mary Ranjitha, Sr.Mary Beena and Sr.Mary Hema were present for this mega function.

The programme started with the ceremonial lightening of the lamp accompanied by bhajan sung by students. A cultural banquet was served to the audience. the choir sang Jubilee song, Hymns to Mother Mary and….classical and contemporary dance styles could be envisioned in “Chotti si asha”, the “Ripples of celebration”, “Gurus of peace”, “Panchabhootam” and ” Asatoma satgamaya”. A grand musical “Beacons of goodness” tracing the birth , growth and the charism of the Congregation of the sisters of Notre Dame was presented in a spiritual fervour. The power point presentation formed the background of the program enriching and making it more enticing.

The Chief Guest Mr.U.P.Pani and Provincial Superior of Notre Dame, Sr.Mary Beena thanked the Notre Dame fraternity for the magical touch they had provided to the august gathering.

Eleven Jubilarians- seven teachers, two office staff members and two supporting staff members were awarded mementos for their committed service during the past 25 glorious years.

Vote of thanks was proposed by Principal Sr.Mary Alice. She thanked one and all for being a part of Notre Dame family. She urged all to be committed global citizens to preserve our mother earth with tender love and to practice universal brotherhood.

The Grand Finale was Asathoma Sat Gamaya, a dance drama depicting the victory of the wise over the wicked in the perennial war within and without amidst the unique virtues of our motherland. In the end, everyone stood up for the National Anthem. The crowd dispersed with happiness in their hearts, smiles on their faces and with vision and mission for future.

Teacher’s Day Celebration! – A cherry on the cake

“Tell me and  I forget
Teach me and I may remember
Involve me and I learn”

- Benjamin Franklin

2015 being the Silver Jubilee year of Notre Dame Badarpur, a sumptuous ‘A la Carte’ of programmes have been laid for the students just to mention a few – Turn Coat, Group/Solo dance, Group/Solo singing, Rangoli, Radio Show, Elocution, Street Play, Poster-making, G.K.Quiz, Collage, Poetry, Recitation, Folk dance, Kite making etc, but the cherry on the cake was of course, the Teacher’s day celebration.

It started with a spiritual prayer service invoking God’s blessing on all the teachers of Notre dame. Students through different programmes acknowledged the great contribution made by the teachers. They portrayed the Notre Dame Gurus as spiritual leaders who were not only preparing them to earn their livelihood but also taught them to live life fruitfully and emerge as responsible citizens of the world. The teachers were thanked by the principal, Sr.Mary Alice and Headmistress, Sr. Mary Reena for their unconditional love and support to the institution. Cards, bouquets, games, gifts and lunch followed the glittering gems in abundance.

Parents Teachers Meeting on 17th July for the Pre-School Children’s Parents

Parent Teacher Meeting was conducted on 17th July for the pre school children’s parents. The meeting started with a prayer song by the school choir. After welcoming the parents, Sister Reena, the Primary Headmistress gave a talk on the psychological growth and development of children of pre school age, and the ill effects of it if not monitored in the right direction.

Sister stressed on the crucial periods of various areas of learning the stimulation required for growth for each of those.

  • For order – 2 to 4 years
  • Emotional growth – 0 to 2 years
  • Language – 0 to 6 years
  • Maths/logic – 1 to 4 years
  • Music – 3 to 10 years

These are scientific findings. Some of the other points stressed are :

  • the need to have a happy atmosphere at home.
  • need to have a good relationship between the parents, between the
    parents and the child and to be role models for their children.
  • need to have guidance, direction and discipline from early years on.
  • the need to plan developmental activities for Children’s emotional and physical growth and development.
  • need to respect the child, his/her views and see that the child is
    taught to respect the elders and others.
  • need to instill faith in God and love love of Go, also to have respect
    for other religions.
  • the need to have self-confidence in dealing with issues from a young
    age.

Finally it is essential that Children of this age have the three ‘A’s of happiness

  • Acceptance
  • Appreciation and
  • Accomplishment for proper life of happiness and well being.

Sister also dealt with how to guide their Children with teaching of letters and numbers Concepts.

Teachers Mrs.Florence, Mrs. Meena and Mrs. Shubha also gave suggestions and helpful hints to the parents. At the end of the meeting parents were allowed to meet their child’s class teachers.

St.Julie’s Feast Celebrated

‘How Good is the good God’ is proclaimed by St.Julie Billiart, foundress of Notre Dame Institutions throughout the world. Her birthday was on 12th July, Sunday. Notre Dame staff and students celebrated it with great love and fervour on Monday 13th of July 2015.

The program started with the inspirational life history of St.Julie Billiart who believed in the simplicity of vision and joy in communication with all people, the goodness of God specially to those who are deprived or in any need.

A devotional prayer dance was staged, girls dressed in pure white and golden hue, adorned with sunflowers, swayed in unison as if depicting to the words – “Like a sunflower , that follows every movement of the sun, So I Turn towards you to follow you, my God. In simplicity… charity… honesty….. fidelity… I follow”

Special prayers were offered by the student council for the Notre Dame family members and for the people all over the world.

The campus echoed with laughter and cheer as prizes were announced for elocution, dance and the most coveted Sweta Memorial award. This award was rightfully bagged by Abhilasha of Std. IX B.

All the students looked happy with “munch” to munch.

Talk on Thalassemia

Tuesday 5th May, the students of Class XI and XII attended a seminar on Thalassemia which is a deadly genetic blood disorder. The talk was conducted by Rtn.Tejinder Singh, Rotary Club of Delhi, Safdurjung. It surely is said that “a journey of thousand miles begins with a single step”. And the first step has surely been taken. A ten minutes docu-drama ‘Kaash’was screened and an interactive session followed. The head girl Joslia thanked the Principal and the Rotary Club as the students felt that they were now much more aware on this topic.

‘Thoughtonics’ ( A seminar for students of Std. XI A and XII A)

On 30th of April, 2015 a seminar on ‘Thoughtonics’ was conducted for students of Std. XI A and XII A by Professor D.D.Sharma.

According to Prof.D.D.Sharma the statement ‘Laws of Physics’ should be changed to ‘Laws of nature’. He believes that faster than light is ‘Thought’. The basis of Thoughtonics were
(i) Mass energy
(ii) Space Time
(iii) Laws of nature
(iv) Thought

He urged the students to think more rationally for he said that Newtonian matured in two centuries and passed the baton to Einsteinian. The right time is ‘NOW’ for the Einsteinian to pass the baton – but what is that which will hold the next baton?

A task for the present students and future scientists to probe into!
