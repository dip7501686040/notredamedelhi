Fire Safety Awareness Training

On 16th of April 2015, fire safety awareness training was given to the students of Notre Dame by the C.I.S.F. Officers of N.T.P.C., Badarpur. They enlightened the students with many facts regarding fire and how to manage it in times of disaster and emergency.

The students were educated on LPG cylinder used at home and the points to be kept in mind because we all know “Fire is a good friend but a bad master”, hence needs to be kept under control.

Seminar for Teachers

“It is the supreme art of the teacher to awaken joy in creative expression and knowledge”                             - Albert Einstein 

On the 6th of April 2015, the new academic session started at Notre Dame with a seminar for the teachers. Sr.Mary Reena (Head Mistress) led everyone present into prayer and later made all aware regarding the expectation of students and parents.

Principal Sr.Mary Alice proclaimed that every academic year  begins with expectation and great zest both for the teachers as well as students. We should make concerted effort to keep it up throughout the year. She also asked the faculty to make a code of conduct for the students and a parallel one for the teachers too. While translating policies into action, the teachers ought to practice what they preach and be apt ‘ROLE MODELS’!

Notre Dame School Silver Jubilee Celebration (1990……….2015……… and beyond…….)

This year is more special than the other ones. As on 29th of November 2014, the Notre Dame family shared a great sense of pride as they all celebrated the Silver Jubilee of their beloved institution.

The august occasion initiated with the auspicious lighting of the lamp followed by readings from the Holy scriptures. Rev. Fr. Anthony Williams blessed the school, staff and students. Songs and Bhajans echoed all around.

The pride felt in the heart and soul of each Notre Dame School member was immense when Mr.A.K.Jha (A.G.M. – HR) on behalf of the G.M., Mr. Debashish Sarkar declared the Siver Jubilee open. The band display and the school choir added to the jubilance. Sr. Mary Ranjita the foundress Principal unveiled the Silver Jubilee Logo which portrayed the unity of mind, body and soul. The logo suggested to all – to create a world without boundaries.

Sr. Mary Alice expressed her thanks to all the previous principals – Sr, Mary Ranjita, Sr. Mary Beena and Sr. Mary Hema  for their contribution. She made all familiar to the different milestones achieved in the past glorious 25 years. She thanked the N.T.P.C. authorities, staff and students also for their unconditional support.

The students put up a variety of items each of which unequivocally established the universally recognised fact that ventures of such magnitude are seldom successful unless we have mass involvement. Dances with different themes were showcased,  just to mention a few – fusion dance, prop dance, east meets west dance, senoritta dance, umbrella and Japanese fan dance etc held the spectators spell bound.

The play ‘ideal Husband’ by Oscar Wilde demonstrated the pivotal thought of an ideal husband which has been an inherent component of various matrimonial philosophies since time immemorial. The subtle wit and humor was enjoyed very well.

A sensitively rendered ‘TALK SHOW’ had the audiences catching glimpses of the milestones attained so far.

In a touching gesture Sr. Mary Reena ( Headmistress) expressed her pleasure to be a part of such an historic event. In her address she felicitated staff and students on their commendable performance in academics and activities. She felt it was only possible because of the commitment and dedication of the Principal, staff, parents and students.

The finale was the school song as all sent a silent prayer to the almighty above to keep the Notre Dame School flag flying high!

Bal – Utsav

Children’s day is celebrated to commemorate the birth anniversary of “Chacha Nehru”, the first Prime Minister of India.

On 14th of November Notre Dame Teachers could be seen in ‘Haute Couture’ attires ready to entertain and pamper the kids in the campus. The day started with a prayer by Sister Mary Reena, our Head Mistress, by invoking God’s blessings on Children all around the world.

Speaking about the function that followed, it was a blend of traditional and contemporary music and dance starting from Kashmir’s ‘Bhumro Bhumro….’ to Kanyakumari’s ‘Kolaveri D’. The finale was an orchestra band with the latest numbers that send the students tapping foot and dancing. It was a day of loads of fun that every child truly deserve. Principal Sister Mary Alice surprised the students with a group song.

The day ended with stuffing the tummy
With sweets and savories yummy
Kids returned home with faces sunny
And thoughts happy and funny.
